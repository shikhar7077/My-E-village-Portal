Jamkhed Gram Panchayat

Nestled in the heart of Maharashtra, Jamkhed stands as a vibrant testament to the spirit of rural India. As the backbone of this serene community, the Jamkhed Gram Panchayat is a vital institution that plays an integral role in shaping the destiny of our village.

  • Local Self-Government

    Gram Panchayats are the lowest tier of the rural local self-government system in India. They are responsible for administering and governing rural areas and villages.

  • Elected Representatives

    Gram Panchayats are composed of elected representatives known as Panchayat Members. These representatives are elected by the eligible voters in the village through periodic elections.

  • Autonomy

    Gram Panchayats have a degree of administrative and financial autonomy. They can make decisions on various local issues, including infrastructure development, social welfare programs, and resource allocation.

  • Devolvement of Powers

    Gram Panchayats have been granted specific powers and functions by the Constitution and relevant state legislation. These powers include taxation, fund management, and the implementation of various developmental schemes.

  • Funds and Finance

    Gram Panchayats receive funds from various sources, including the central and state governments, as well as local revenue sources such as taxes and fees. They manage and allocate these funds for local development projects.
